The Silent Toll of Sitting

Prolonged sitting can lead to muscle imbalances, primarily "sleepy" glutes and shortened hamstrings. This protocol is your secret weapon to counteract these effects, boost energy, and prevent pain.

8+ Hours

Average daily sitting time for office workers, leading to muscle deactivation.

Sleepy Glutes

Underactive gluteal muscles from constant pressure and lack of use.

Tight Hammies

Hamstrings shorten in a seated position, contributing to lower back pain.

Seated Piriformis Stretch

Opens up tight glutes from prolonged sitting, providing immediate relief.

  • How: Sit upright, place one ankle over the opposite knee. Lean forward with a straight back until you feel a stretch in the glute.
  • When: Every 2-3 hours.
  • Duration: Hold for 1 minute on each side.

Hamstring Stretch on Chair

Prevents the shortening of hamstrings which can pull on the lower back.

  • How: Sit at your chair's edge. Extend one leg straight, heel on the floor. Lean forward with a flat back.
  • When: Morning & Night, or during breaks.
  • Duration: Hold for 1 minute on each side.

Glute Bridges

Your secret therapy to reactivate "sleepy" glutes and build foundational strength.

  • How: Lie on your back, knees bent. Lift your hips by pushing through your heels, squeezing glutes at the top.
  • When: Before bed or after a long sitting session.
  • Reps: 2 sets of 15 repetitions.
ü¶ø

Standing Hamstring Curls

A simple, desk-friendly way to warm up hamstrings and improve blood flow.

  • How: While standing, bring one heel towards your glute. Squeeze the hamstring for a second at the top.
  • When: During work breaks or even on calls.
  • Reps: 2 sets of 15 repetitions each leg.
